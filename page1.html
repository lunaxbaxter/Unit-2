Releasing Pets Into The Wild

What Can Happen To The Animals

Although it, of course, doesn't happen one hundred percent of the time, there are cases where the animal that has been let loose manages to survive on its own and reproduce.

In these rare cases where the animal survives and establishes themselves in a non-native environment or ecosystems, they become known as an invasive species. According to the National Invasive Species Information Center or the NISIC, an invasive species is defined and can be any non-native plant, animal or pathogen that establishes itself in the surrounding ecosystem.

When the invasive species repopulates and its population becomes steady and unwavering, it causing stress in its living habitats and can even wipe out or severely damage indigenous species that were there before it.
